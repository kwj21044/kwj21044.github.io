About



Hello!

I'm Kevin, a second year student at Harvard Law School. I graduated from Columbia University in 2013, where I studied economics and computer science. In a past life I worked as a software developer at Amazon and a consultant in New York City. Nowadays, my legal interests include international arbitration and IP transactions, and I will be working at Steptoe & Johnson and Cleary Gottlieb in Summer 2017.

Despite my legal career, I am still a builder at heart. I'm interested in continuing to explore topics in technology such as digital fabrication, embedded programming, open source development, machine learning, digital privacy regulation, among others. Check out my work from Professor Gershenfeld's amazing MAS 863 How to Make (Almost) Anything.

How to Design (Almost) Anything is a one-semester technique-based class is for graduate or undergraduate students without a design background to enable more effective collaboration with designers and the ability to apply the foundations of design to any discipline.
